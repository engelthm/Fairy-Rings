The Ring Beyond Time

Fairy Rings

Step Between the Toadstools...

...and you may just stumble upon the traces of dancing fairies, a portal to a timeless realm, or the meeting place of devil-worshiping witches.

All refer, of course, to the same, incredible phenomenon — fairy rings.

Join the Mycological Society of London as they uncover the fact, and folklore, surrounding this centuries-old mystery.

In the Circles of Sorcerers

Though as widespread across Europe as the stories of fairy rings themselves, Tricholoma orirubens, or blushing Tricholoma, is rare.

Perhaps it is no wonder then, that its manifestation in Witches’ Circles has spurred folklore throughout Germanic culture.

Nornahringir in Iceland, ronds de sorcières in France, and Hexenringe in Germany; where it is said that on Sankt-Walpurgisnacht malevolent witches gather, dancing around rings of fungi as they consort with the devil.

Dancing with the 'Other Crowd'

Clitocybe rivulosa, aptly referred to as the Fool’s Funnel, is deadly poisonous. If Celtic folklore is also to be believed, however, then stepping into a circle of these mushrooms could lead to a fate far worse.

Forced to dance for all of eternity, losing your name, perception of time, or turning invisible are but only a few consequences of infringing upon fairy territory.

Called by many names—the Other Crowd, Fair Folk, Hidden People, or Aos si—their rings of mushrooms are a devious trap, laying in wait for ignorant humans.
